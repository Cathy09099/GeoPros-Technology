Namibia's Resources & Uranium Technologies

Understanding the impact of uranium extraction on Namibia's natural wealth and how new technologies could help

Namibia’s Natural Wealth

Namibia is rich in mineral resources, including uranium, diamonds, copper, and zinc. These have historically powered the nation’s economy and driven foreign investment. Uranium alone accounts for over 10% of global supply and contributes billions in export value annually.

However, traditional mining has also placed pressure on Namibia’s environment, water resources, and rural communities. The way forward requires more efficient, sustainable approaches.

Namibia’s diverse natural resources support development

Types of Uranium Mining in Namibia

Open-Pit Mining

Used at Rössing and Husab mines. Involves removing large surface layers, resulting in high land disturbance, dust, and water use. Economically effective but environmentally damaging.

In-Situ Recovery (ISR)

Involves injecting solutions underground to dissolve uranium, which is then pumped to the surface. Lower environmental impact, reduced water waste, and no massive open pits.

Biotech & Global Innovation

Russia and others are using bacterial leaching and laser-based mining to improve efficiency. Namibia has the potential to adopt these techniques for cleaner, smarter mining.

Impact of Uranium Mining on Namibia’s Resources

  • Water: Traditional mining consumes large volumes. ISR and biotech methods can reduce this drastically.
  • Land: Open pits destroy surface ecosystems. ISR leaves land largely undisturbed.
  • Energy: Conventional methods use more energy. Biotech and ISR are more energy-efficient.
  • Air Quality: Dust and emissions are reduced with ISR and enclosed leaching methods.
  • Community Health: Cleaner methods reduce exposure to radioactive dust and contaminated runoff.

Technology That Could Benefit Namibia

Namibia is poised to adopt advanced technologies to enhance its uranium sector. These include:

  • Bacterial Leaching: Uses natural bacteria to break down uranium from low-grade ores and tailings, reducing the need for harmful chemicals.
  • Laser Separation: A Russian technique that can isolate uranium with high precision and less waste.
  • Automation & AI: Improves safety, reduces labor risks, and enhances efficiency.

Introducing these technologies would allow Namibia to remain globally competitive while protecting its people and ecosystems.
