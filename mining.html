Mining Methods in Namibia

Comprehensive overview of uranium extraction techniques used in Namibian mines

Overview

Namibia uses a variety of uranium mining methods based on deposit depth, rock type, and ore grade. Open-pit mining is dominant, but In-Situ Recovery (ISR) is emerging as a modern, lower-impact option.

Overview of mining operations in Namibia

Open-Pit Mining

Used at Rössing and Husab mines for near-surface uranium. Involves removal of overburden, blasting, excavation, and transport to processing plants.

  • Advantages: High production, easier maintenance
  • Disadvantages: Significant land disruption and dust generation

Open-pit mining at Rössing Mine

Underground Mining

Less common in Namibia, but used where uranium is deep underground. Methods include room-and-pillar and longwall mining.

Challenges: Higher costs, safety requirements

Underground uranium mining operation

In-Situ Recovery (ISR)

This innovative method involves dissolving uranium in-place using a leaching solution, then pumping it to the surface for processing. It minimizes surface disturbance.

  • Benefits: Low surface impact, reduced waste
  • Risks: Potential groundwater contamination

Future of Mining

Namibia is exploring environmentally friendly technologies like ISR and biotech leaching to reduce costs and environmental impact while accessing lower-grade ores.
