About Uranium in Namibia

Understanding Namibia's position in the global uranium industry

Overview

Namibia is one of the world's leading uranium producers, contributing approximately 10% of global uranium production. The country's uranium industry has been a cornerstone of its economy since the 1970s, providing significant export revenue and employment opportunities.

Located in the heart of the Namib Desert, Namibia's uranium deposits are among the most significant in the world, with the country ranking as the third-largest uranium producer globally.

Current Mining Operations

Rössing Mine

Owner: Rio Tinto (68.6%)

Type: Open-pit operation

Status: Active since 1976

World's longest-running open-pit uranium mine, located in the Namib Desert near Swakopmund.

Husab Mine

Owner: Swakop Uranium (90%)

Type: Open-pit operation

Status: Active since 2016

One of the world's largest uranium mines by production capacity, with potential for 15+ years of operation.

Langer Heinrich Mine

Owner: Paladin Energy

Type: Heap leach operation

Status: Care and maintenance

Currently on care and maintenance, with potential for restart based on market conditions.

Economic Impact

The uranium industry plays a crucial role in Namibia's economy:

  • Export Revenue: Generates billions in foreign currency
  • Employment: Direct employment of over 8,000 people
  • Government Revenue: Significant tax and royalty contributions
  • Infrastructure: Development of roads, ports, and utilities
  • Local Communities: Skills development and community programs
