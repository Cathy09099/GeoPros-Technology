Geological Foundations of Namibian Uranium

Exploring the ancient rocks and formations that hold Namibia's uranium treasures. How Namibia became one of the world's leading producers of Uranium.

Geological Overview

Namibia's uranium deposits are primarily hosted in the Namib Desert, within the Central Zone of the Damara Orogen. These deposits formed through complex geological processes over millions of years, creating some of the world's most significant uranium concentrations.

The geological setting is characterized by granitic intrusions, metamorphic rocks, and sedimentary formations that have been subjected to extensive weathering and groundwater interaction.

Ancient granite formations in the Namib Desert

The Damara Orogen

Formation History

Age: 750-450 million years ago

Process: Continental collision and orogeny

The Damara Orogen formed during the Pan-African orogeny, creating the structural framework that would later host uranium mineralization.

Rock Types

Granite: Rössing and Husab granites

Metamorphic: Schists and gneisses

Complex intrusive and metamorphic rocks provide the source and host for uranium mineralization.

Structural Control

Faults: Control fluid flow

Fractures: Pathway for mineralization

Structural features control the distribution and concentration of uranium deposits.

Types of Uranium Deposits

Primary Deposits

Location: Rössing, Husab mines

Host Rock: Granite and alaskite

Minerals: Uraninite, betafite

Formation: Magmatic processes

  • Formed during granite crystallization
  • High-grade uranium concentrations
  • Associated with accessory minerals
  • Require complex processing

Secondary Deposits

Location: Langer Heinrich, Valencia

Host Rock: Calcrete, sediments

Minerals: Carnotite, tyuyamunite

Formation: Groundwater processes

  • Formed by groundwater leaching
  • Surface and near-surface deposits
  • Easier to process
  • Lower grade but large tonnage

Key Geological Features

Granite Intrusions

Large granite bodies that contain primary uranium mineralization, formed during the late stages of the Damara Orogen.

Weathering Profiles

Deep weathering in the arid climate has created extensive weathering profiles that concentrate uranium minerals.
